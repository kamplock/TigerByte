ABC

You will be given three integers A, B and C. The numbers will not be given in that exact order, but we do know that A is less than B and B less than C. In order to make for a more pleasant viewing, we want to rearrange them in a given order.

Arrangement

One of the things that programming competition organizers have to do is decide which team should be in which room. There are N rooms and M teams. The rooms are similar in size, so it is best for teams to be divided into rooms as evenly as possible. For example, if there are N = 3 rooms and M = 8 teams, then it is best to put 3 teams in one room, 3 teams in another room, and then the last 2 teams in the last room.

Cold-puter Science

We’re not going to sugar-coat it: Chicago’s winters can be rough. The temperatures sometimes dip to uncomfortable levels and, after last year’s “polar vortex”, the University of Chicago Weather Service wants to find out exactly how bad the winter was. More specifically, they are interested in knowing the total number of days in which the temperature was below zero degrees Celsius.

Crypt Kicker

A common but insecure method of encrypting text is to permute the letters of the alphabet. That is, in the text, each letter of the alphabet is consistently replaced by some other letter. So as to ensure that the encryption is reversible, no two letters are replaced by the same letter. Your task is to decrypt several encoded lines of text, assuming that each line uses a different set of replacements, and that all words in the decrypted text are from a dictionary of known words.

About Us

Welcome to the Doane Programming Team! Here are some key points about us.

  • 1 programming competition per semester
  • Kattis & TigerByte are our main websites for practice problems
  • Python & Java are our main languages

As members of the Doane Programming Team, Olivia Navarro & Kamryn Plock have developed TigerByte, a full-stack web application for our Senior Capstone Project, dedicated to bridging the gap in accessing programming problem sets. Navigating through an extensive paper binder of programming problems can be a daunting task, especially when specific challenges related to data structures, difficulty levels, or problem names are desired. TigerByte aims to address this challenge by providing efficient access to programming problem sets.
